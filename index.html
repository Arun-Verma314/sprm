Lecture 01 Topic: Sequences

The Rigorous Limit of a Sequence: Intuition vs. Rigour

An introduction detailing why mathematical intuition alone fails to guarantee sequence limits. Features the "Foggy Road" structural analogy to decode the formal $\epsilon$-$K$ definition.

Core Definition Recap:

A sequence $(a_n)$ converges to $L \in \mathbb{R}$ if for every $\epsilon > 0$, there exists $K \in \mathbb{N}$ such that for all $n \geq K$:

$$|a_n - L| < \epsilon$$
